Shared Imaging Equipment at Purdue:

  • Campus core facilities provide access to an inverted Zeiss LSM 710 state-of-the-art laser scanning confocal microscope equipped with laser lines at 405, 458, 488, 514, 561 and 633 nm and a 34 channel spectral detector that enables simultaneous imaging of multiple fluorescent proteins, well-suited to imaging fixed cochlear immunofluorescence samples (with a step size of 0.25 µm; Frame Size 1024 x 1024; scan speed 7; Averaging of 2 with 8-bit depth).
  • Super-Resolution Imaging Lab on campus provides superior resolution of synaptic puncta using the Leica SP5 TCS STED CW system optimized with 2-color STED CW sample preparation (Atto 425, antibody or streptavidin conjugated • Pacific Orange, antibody conjugated). Leica SP5 STED TCS CW system offers up to 50 nm in XY with a step size of 40 nm Super Z. Acquired images are processed, visualized, segmented & interpreted using Imaris software (Biplane’s core scientific software module) provides a complete set of features for working with three- and four-dimensional multi-channel images of any size, from a few megabytes to multiple gigabytes in size.
